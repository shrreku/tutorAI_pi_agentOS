# Product

## Register

product

## Users

StudyAgent is for individual students using a personal learning workspace repeatedly over time. They arrive to upload learning material, ask focused questions, follow study plans, inspect concept relationships, and review generated learning artifacts while staying oriented in one workspace.

## Product Purpose

StudyAgent turns course material and notes into an adaptive tutoring workspace. It combines source ingestion, tutor chat, a graph/whiteboard study map, study-state planning, and artifact review so a student can move from raw material to structured understanding without losing context. Success means the student can quickly see what to study next, trust why the system is recommending it, and move between explanation, evidence, graph context, and practice artifacts without friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, rigorous, adaptive. The interface should feel steady and clear under repeated study use, precise enough for serious academic work, and responsive to each student's current understanding.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI chat, classroom LMS patterns, Notion-clone layouts, flashy gamified study apps, and dense admin dashboards. The product should not feel decorative, corporate, overbuilt, or like a pile of disconnected panels.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the student oriented: every major surface should make the current notebook, active learning context, and next useful action easy to locate.
2. Make rigor legible: provenance, graph relationships, study state, and artifacts should expose reasoning without overwhelming the learning flow.
3. Let the tutor and workspace cooperate: chat, graph/whiteboard, source status, and artifact viewer should feel like parts of one study loop.
4. Prioritize repeated-use calm: dense information is allowed, but rhythm, grouping, and restrained emphasis should prevent cognitive fatigue.
5. Adapt without surprise: system updates, generated artifacts, and study-plan changes should feel transparent, reversible where appropriate, and grounded in the student's materials.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal accessibility target is set yet. The interface should still be easy to use and understand, with clear labels, readable contrast, keyboard-reachable controls where practical, and status cues that do not depend on color alone.
